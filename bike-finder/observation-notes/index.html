Buying a Trek Bike

  1. Typed in trek.com
  2. Scrolled through site
  3. Went to "Bikes" on navigation bar
  4. Saw different types
  5. Selected "Help Me Choose"
  6. Starts Quiz
  7. Quiz asked about how we'd be using the bike, price, body positioning and gave 3 suggestions on bikes that would be best given those preferences
  8. Looks at cheapest bike first
  9. Looks at pictures of the bike
  10. Tries alternate color
  11. Reads description and key features
  12. Scrolls down to features, skips past technical information besides material, frame type and weight
  13. Decides this bike has everything he wants
  14. Determines sizing based on height
  15. Browses reviews looking at Pros and Cons, sees it is highly rates
  16. Goes to lowest review first but the criticisms aren't relevant
  17. Looks at FAQs
  18. Wants to compare to other models
  19. Compares DS2 model to DS1
  20. Looks at comparison table
  21. Now looks at DS1 because it is cheaper and seems very similar
  22. Decides to purchase DS1
  23. Has option to ship or buy in store
  24. Selects "Find Retailer" and inputs zip code
  25. Finds a bike store nearby and selects "Get Address"
  26. Is taken to Google Maps and given directions to the nearby bike store

Overall the logic of the design made sense -- logical steps a consumer would make when buying a product. User experience was designed around this process --> started with general details, more specific details, then looking at what others thought, allowing you to compare to other models.
